It has a population of 325,671 and an area of 103,000km2 (40,000sq mi), making it the most sparsely populated country in Europe. The capital and largest city is Reykjavik; the surrounding areas in the South-West of the country are home to two thirds of the population. Iceland is volcanically and geologically active. The interior consists mainly of a plateau characterised by sand and alva fields, mountains and glaciers, while many glacial rivers flow to the sea threough the lowlands. Iceland is warmed by the Gulf Stream and has a temperate climate despite a hight latitude just outside of the Arctic Circle.

History

According to both Landnamabok and Islendingabok, Celtic monks known as the Papar lived in Iceland before the Norse settlers arrived, possibly members of the Hiberno-Scottish mission. Recent archaeological excavations have revealed the ruins of a cabin in Hafnir on the Reykjanes peninsula. Carbon dating indicates that it was abandoned somewhere betwen 779 and 880, suggesting that Iceland was populated well before 874. This archaeological find may also indicate that the monks left Iceland before the Norse arrived.

Iceland on the Carta Marina by Olaus Magnus

Swedish Viking explorer Gardar Svavarsson was the first to circumnavigate Iceland in 870 and establish that it was an island. He stayed over winter and build a house in Husavik. Gardar departed the following summer but one of his men, Nattfari, decided to stay behind with two slaves. Nattfari settled in what is now known as Nattfaravik and became the first permanent resident of Iceland.

Cuisine

Much of Icelands's cuisine is based on fish, lamb, and dairy products, with little to no utilisation of herbs and spices. Due to the island's climate, fruits and vegetables and not generally a component of traditional dishes, althow the use of greenhouses has made them more common in contemporary food. Porramatur is a selection of traditional cuisine consisting of many dishes, and is usually consumed around the month of Porri.

Breakfast usually consists of pancakes, cereal, fruit, and coffee, while lunch may take the form of a smorgasbord. The main meal of the day for most icelanders is diner, which usualy involves fish or lamb as the main course. Iceland is a representative democracy and a parliamentary republic. The modern parliament, Alpingi (English: Althing), was founded in 1845 as an advisory body to the danish monarch. It was widely seen as a re-establishment of the assembly founded in 930 in the Commonwealth period and suspended in 1799. Consequently, "it is arguably the wordls's oldest parliamentary democracy." It currently has 63 members, elected for a maximum period of four years, with no term limit. The elections for president, the Althing and local municipal coucils are all help separately every four years.

Municipalities of Iceland

The municipalities of Iceland are local administrative areaas in Iceland that provide a number of services to their inhabitants such as kindergartens, elementary schools, waste management, social services, public housing, public transportation, services to senior citizens and handicapped people. They also govern zoning and can voluntary take on additional functions if they have the budget for it. The autonomy of municipalities over their own matters is guaranteed by the constitution of Iceland.
